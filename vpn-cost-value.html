Understanding the Australia Tax

Australians consistently pay higher prices for digital goods and services compared to US or European customers purchasing identical products. This price discrimination‚Äîcolloquially called the "Australia tax"‚Äîaffects software, streaming services, games, cloud storage, and digital subscriptions.

Examples from 2026 pricing research:

  • Adobe Creative Cloud: AU$79.99/month vs US$54.99/month (45% higher)
  • Microsoft Office 365: AU$119/year vs US$69.99/year (70% higher)
  • Video games: AU$109.95 vs US$59.99 (83% higher)
  • Streaming services: AU pricing typically 20-40% higher for identical content

Using a VPN to purchase from US or European stores can save hundreds of dollars annually on digital subscriptions and purchases. However, note that some companies' terms of service prohibit this practice, and payment method verification sometimes prevents it. The legality in Australia is grey‚Äîit's not explicitly illegal, but it may violate service agreements.

Non-Financial Value: Privacy and Security

Whilst harder to quantify, the privacy and security benefits represent significant value:

  • Protection from ISP surveillance: Your ISP cannot build detailed profiles of your online behaviour for advertising or sell your browsing data to third parties. Under Australia's data retention laws, they must store metadata, but VPNs make that metadata useless for detailed surveillance.
  • Public Wi-Fi security: Every time you use caf√©, airport, or hotel Wi-Fi without a VPN, you risk man-in-the-middle attacks, credential theft, and session hijacking. One prevented identity theft incident alone justifies years of VPN subscriptions.
  • Protection from government overreach: Whilst Australian authorities can legally request ISP data, VPNs prevent that data from being useful for detailed activity reconstruction. This privacy protection has intangible but real value in a surveillance-heavy environment.
  • Peace of mind: Knowing your online activities are private and secure provides psychological value that's difficult to quantify but genuinely valuable.

Should You Use Free VPNs?

The question "how much does a VPN cost?" tempts many Australians towards free alternatives. After evaluating dozens of free VPN services over my career, my professional advice is straightforward: avoid free VPNs except for very specific, limited use cases.

Why Free VPNs Are Problematic

VPN operation requires significant infrastructure investment‚Äîservers worldwide, bandwidth costs, software development, security audits, customer support. If you're not paying with money, you're paying another way, typically through:

  • Data collection and selling: Many free VPNs collect detailed browsing data and sell it to advertising networks, data brokers, or worse. This defeats the entire purpose of using a VPN for privacy.
  • Injected advertising: Some free VPNs inject advertisements into websites you visit, modifying web pages to display ads that generate revenue for the provider.
  • Malware distribution: Security research has documented free VPN apps containing malware, spyware, or other malicious code that compromises user devices.
  • Bandwidth theft: Some free VPNs use your device's bandwidth to route other users' traffic, essentially turning your computer into an exit node for their network without your informed consent.
  • Weak security: Free VPNs often use outdated encryption, have poor security practices, and lack basic protections like kill switches and DNS leak prevention.

Legitimate Free VPN Options (Limited Exceptions)

A few free VPNs operate ethically with transparent business models:

  • Proton VPN Free: Offers genuinely free tier with no data caps, supported by paid subscriptions. Limited to slower servers and fewer locations, but trustworthy privacy practices. Acceptable for basic protection on limited budgets.
  • Windscribe Free: Provides 10GB monthly free data with email registration. Legitimate company that monetises through paid upgrades rather than data collection. Useful for occasional VPN needs.

Even these legitimate free options have significant limitations‚Äîslower speeds, fewer servers, data caps, and limited features. They're acceptable for experimenting with VPN technology or very light usage, but inadequate for regular protection.

‚ö†Ô∏è Mia's Free VPN Warning

I've consulted on numerous cases where Australians using free VPNs discovered their "privacy protection" was actually selling their browsing data, injecting malware, or worse. The adage "if you're not paying for the product, you are the product" applies perfectly to free VPNs. Saving $10/month isn't worth compromising the privacy you're trying to protect. If budget is genuinely constrained, use Proton VPN's free tier from a reputable company rather than unknown free VPNs with questionable motives.

Maximising Your VPN Value

Once you've invested in a quality VPN subscription, maximise your return through strategic use and taking advantage of included features many users overlook.

Features Worth Using

  • Kill switch: Ensure this is enabled to prevent accidental data exposure if your VPN disconnects. Essential security feature that many users forget to activate.
  • Split tunnelling: Route only necessary traffic through VPN whilst allowing local services direct connections. Improves performance whilst maintaining protection where it matters.
  • Ad-blocking features: Many VPNs include ad-blocking (NordVPN's CyberSec, Surfshark's CleanWeb) that improve browsing experience and security beyond basic VPN protection.
  • Multiple device protection: Install your VPN on all eligible devices. If you're paying for 5-6 simultaneous connections, use them all‚Äîprotect your phone, computer, tablet, and consider router installation for comprehensive coverage.
  • Specialised servers: Use P2P servers for torrenting, streaming servers for Netflix/Disney+, and double-VPN servers when you need extra privacy layers. You're paying for these features‚Äîtake advantage of them.

Subscription Timing Strategies

Smart subscription timing can save additional money:

  • Wait for sales: Major VPN providers run promotional sales around Black Friday, Cyber Monday, New Year, and other holidays. These sales often offer 40-70% discounts off standard pricing.
  • Use money-back guarantees: Test performance from your Australian connection during the guarantee period (typically 30 days). If performance is unsatisfactory, get a full refund and try alternatives risk-free.
  • Subscribe to longer terms: Two-year plans offer dramatically better per-month pricing than monthly or even annual subscriptions. If you're committed to VPN use, longer terms maximise value.
  • Share family plans: Some VPNs (particularly Surfshark with unlimited devices) allow family sharing. Split the cost amongst household members to reduce individual expense whilst maintaining protection.
