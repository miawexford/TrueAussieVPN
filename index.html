Mia's Plain English Explanation

Think of a VPN as a secure tunnel between your device and the internet. The websites you visit see the VPN server's IP address, not yours, protecting your identity and location.

Common Questions About VPNs

Expert answers to what Australians ask most

Is VPN legal in Australia? ‚ñº

Yes, using a VPN is completely legal in Australia. Australians have the right to use VPN services to protect their privacy and security online. However, using a VPN to engage in illegal activities remains illegal‚Äîa VPN doesn't make unlawful behaviour lawful. You can legally use a VPN for privacy protection, accessing geo-restricted content, securing your connection on public Wi-Fi, and protecting your data from surveillance. The confusion often arises because some countries like China restrict VPN use, but Australia has no such restrictions.

Read my detailed legal analysis: VPN legality in Australia

Do I need a VPN on my iPhone or Android phone? ‚ñº

If you ever use public Wi-Fi, access sensitive information online, or care about your privacy, then yes, you need a VPN on your phone. Mobile devices are particularly vulnerable because we use them everywhere‚Äîcaf√©s, shopping centres, airports, hotels. Every time you connect to public Wi-Fi without a VPN, you're potentially exposing your passwords, banking information, and personal data to hackers on the same network.

Setting up a VPN on iPhone or Android is straightforward. Most quality VPN providers offer dedicated apps that connect with a single tap. Once configured, you can set your VPN to automatically activate when you join public Wi-Fi networks, providing seamless protection without thinking about it.

How much does a VPN cost in Australia? ‚ñº

VPN pricing in Australia typically ranges from $2 to $15 per month (AUD), depending on the provider and subscription length. Premium services like ExpressVPN and NordVPN cost around $10-15 monthly but drop to $8-10 per month with annual plans. Mid-range options like Surfshark and CyberGhost offer excellent value at $5-8 monthly. Budget providers can cost as little as $2-4 per month with long-term commitments.

My advice: Don't choose based solely on price. The cheapest VPN isn't worth it if it's slow, unreliable, or doesn't protect your privacy. Similarly, the most expensive isn't automatically the best. Focus on value‚Äîwhat features, performance, and privacy protection you get for your money. For most Australians, I recommend mid-range to premium services that balance cost with quality.

Can VPN be tracked by government or ISP? ‚ñº

Your ISP can see that you're using a VPN‚Äîthey can identify the encrypted connection to a VPN server‚Äîbut they cannot see what you're doing through that VPN. They cannot track which websites you visit, what data you send or receive, or any details of your online activities. The content of your internet traffic is encrypted and hidden from your ISP.

Regarding government tracking: Australian authorities cannot directly track your VPN-protected internet activities. However, if you're under active investigation, authorities could potentially request data from your VPN provider. This is why choosing a VPN with a genuine no-logs policy and favourable jurisdiction is important. Quality VPNs based outside the Five Eyes alliance (which includes Australia) provide the strongest protection against potential data requests.

Understanding VPN limitations: Can VPN be tracked?

Does VPN work in China or other restrictive countries? ‚ñº

China actively blocks most VPN services, making it challenging but not impossible to use VPNs there. Some VPN providers‚Äîparticularly ExpressVPN, NordVPN, and Surfshark‚Äîhave developed technologies to work in China, though reliability varies. If you're planning to travel to China, set up your VPN before you arrive, as downloading VPN apps from within China is difficult.

The key is choosing a VPN that specifically advertises China compatibility and uses obfuscation technology to disguise VPN traffic as regular HTTPS traffic. Not all VPNs work in China, and those that do may experience periodic disruptions when the Great Firewall is updated. Always have backup VPN apps installed before travelling to restrictive countries.

Does VPN drain battery or slow down internet? ‚ñº

Yes, VPNs do consume additional battery on mobile devices because encryption and maintaining a secure connection requires processing power. However, with modern smartphones and efficient VPN apps, the battery impact is typically modest‚Äîaround 5-15% additional battery drain. The exact impact depends on your VPN protocol (WireGuard uses less battery than OpenVPN), your phone's processor, and how actively you're using the internet.

Regarding internet speed: VPNs do slightly reduce your internet speed because your data takes a longer route (through the VPN server) and requires encryption/decryption. Quality VPNs typically reduce speeds by 10-30%. With Australia's improving internet infrastructure in 2026, this speed reduction is rarely noticeable for browsing, streaming, or general use. Poor-quality VPNs can significantly slow your connection, which is why choosing a reputable provider matters.
